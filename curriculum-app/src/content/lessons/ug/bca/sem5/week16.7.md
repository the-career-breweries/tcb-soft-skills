# Resume Building Masterclass

A resume is a key document in the professional world, serving as a snapshot of your qualifications, skills, experience, and accomplishments. 

Today, we will build a stand-out resume tailored for a fresher that gets you shortlisted.

---

# Features of a Good Resume

Before we start building, here is what makes a resume stand out:
*   **Clickable Links:** All necessary links (Email, LinkedIn, GitHub, Portfolio) are present and clickable.
*   **Concise Experiences:** All projects and internships are mentioned in a concise, bulleted manner.
*   **Categorized Skills:** Skills are categorized (Languages, Tools, Databases, Web Technologies) and mentioned within project descriptions.
*   **Complete General Info:** Name, contact details, and location are clear.
*   **Proof of Work:** Mentions open-source projects, college clubs, and certifications with clickable links.

# The Resume Comparison Test

When reviewing resumes, recruiters look for immediate clarity. Consider the difference:

### ❌ The Bad Resume
*   Messy, multi-column format with graphics that confuse ATS scanners.
*   Vague descriptions like "Responsible for developing the app."
*   Missing demo links; recruiters have to guess if the project actually works.

### ✅ The Good Resume
*   Clean, single-column layout readable by both humans and machines.
*   Action-oriented bullets: "Developed a travel website using React, improving load times by 20%."
*   Clickable GitHub and Live Demo links right next to the project title.

---

# Step 1: General Info & Portfolios

Your contact section must be flawless. Include:
*   **Full Name**
*   **Phone Number** & **Email Address** (Professional format)
*   **LinkedIn Profile (Must)** - Acts as your digital resume to actively connect with recruiters.
*   **GitHub Profile (Must)** - Provides evidence of coding abilities, problem-solving skills, and open-source collaboration. Tech companies rely on it!
*   **Portfolio / Leetcode (Optional but recommended)**

---

# Step 2: The Resume Objective

Since you are entering the industry, your resume should feature an **Objective Statement**. 

An objective is a concise statement outlining your career goals and the type of position you are seeking.

*Example:* "A highly motivated individual with core knowledge of modern web technologies, looking forward to working in a dynamic company where I can cultivate my skills to add value and enhance my problem-solving abilities."

---

# Step 3: Projects & Internships

As a fresher, your projects are your experience. Ensure you have these 5 elements for each:
1.  **Project Name / Role**
2.  **Demo Link / GitHub Repo**
3.  **Dates:** Start and end dates
4.  **Bullet Points:** Use the **STAR Method** (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to write these. Start with **Action Verbs** (Developed, Managed, Built, Integrated).
5.  **Quantify Achievements:** Always use numbers (e.g., "Boosted the app's performance by using multithreading for parallel search queries").
*Ensure consistency of grammar throughout!*

---

# Structure of a Fresher's Resume

For a Tier-1 fresher or non-working graduate, order your sections strictly like this:

1.  **Name, Contact, and Portfolio Links**
2.  **Skills (Tech Stacks & Categories)**
3.  **Education (Latest Degree first, CGPA if good)**
4.  **Projects (Ordered by relevance: Main Projects then Mini Projects)**
5.  **Clubs, Activities & Achievements**

---

# Evaluating Your Resume

Once your draft is ready, you must evaluate it:
1.  **Peer Review:** Request a review from batchmates, friends, or mentors.
2.  **ATS Score Check:**
    *   Open **resumeworded.com**
    *   Copy/Paste a Job Description you want to apply for.
    *   Upload your resume.
    *   *Target:* Ensure your score is **>60 in the skills section**.

---

# Activity: Draft Your Stand-Out Resume

1.  **Format:** Pick a clean, single-column template.
2.  **Sections:** Outline your sections based on the Fresher structure.
3.  **Drafting:** Write your project bullet points using Action Verbs and the STAR method.
4.  **Evaluate:** Run your draft through an ATS scanner later today!

**Task:** You have 30 minutes to draft the first version of your professional fresher resume.
