# Resume Building Masterclass

A resume is a key document in the professional world, serving as a snapshot of your qualifications, skills, experience, and accomplishments. 

Today, we will build a stand-out resume tailored for a fresher that gets you shortlisted.

---

# Features of a Good Resume

Before we start building, here is what makes a resume stand out:
*   **Clickable Links:** All necessary links (Email, LinkedIn, Portfolio) are present and clickable.
*   **Concise Experiences:** All projects, case studies, and internships are mentioned in a concise, bulleted manner.
*   **Categorized Skills:** Skills are categorized (Finance, Accounting, Tools, Soft Skills) and mentioned within project descriptions.
*   **Complete General Info:** Name, contact details, and location are clear.
*   **Proof of Work:** Mentions certifications (e.g., NISM, Excel), case competitions, and college clubs with clickable links.

---

# The Resume Comparison Test

When reviewing resumes, recruiters look for immediate clarity. Consider the difference:

### ❌ The Bad Resume
*   Messy, multi-column format with graphics that confuse ATS scanners.
*   Vague descriptions like "Responsible for managing the finance club."
*   Missing evidence of work; recruiters have to guess your actual proficiency.

### ✅ The Good Resume
*   Clean, single-column layout readable by both humans and machines.
*   Action-oriented bullets: "Managed a budget of ₹50,000 for the finance club, reducing event overheads by 15%."
*   Clickable links to LinkedIn and uploaded certifications.

---

# Step 1: General Info & Portfolios

Your contact section must be flawless. Include:
*   **Full Name**
*   **Phone Number** & **Email Address** (Professional format)
*   **LinkedIn Profile (Must)** - Acts as your digital resume to actively connect with recruiters in the corporate sector.
*   **Online Portfolio (Optional)** - A link to a Google Drive folder containing your financial models, case study presentations, or research papers.

---

# Step 2: The Resume Objective

Since you are entering the industry, your resume should feature an **Objective Statement**. 

An objective is a concise statement outlining your career goals and the type of position you are seeking.

*Example:* "A highly motivated commerce graduate with core knowledge of financial analysis and accounting principles, looking forward to working in a dynamic firm where I can cultivate my skills to add value and enhance my problem-solving abilities."

---

# Step 3: Projects & Internships

As a fresher, your case studies and internships are your experience. Ensure you have these 5 elements for each:
1.  **Project Name / Role** (e.g., "Financial Modeling Intern")
2.  **Company Name / College Competition**
3.  **Dates:** Start and end dates
4.  **Bullet Points:** Use the **STAR Method** (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to write these. Start with **Action Verbs** (Analyzed, Managed, Forecasted, Optimized).
5.  **Quantify Achievements:** Always use numbers (e.g., "Analyzed 5 years of financial data to forecast quarterly revenue growth of 12%").
*Ensure consistency of grammar throughout!*

---

# Structure of a Fresher's Resume

For a commerce fresher, order your sections strictly like this:

1.  **Name, Contact, and Portfolio Links**
2.  **Skills (Finance, Accounting, Tools)**
3.  **Education (Latest Degree first, CGPA if good)**
4.  **Projects & Internships (Ordered by relevance)**
5.  **Clubs, Activities & Achievements**

---

# Evaluating Your Resume

Once your draft is ready, you must evaluate it:
1.  **Peer Review:** Request a review from batchmates, friends, or mentors.
2.  **ATS Score Check:**
    *   Open **resumeworded.com**
    *   Copy/Paste a Job Description you want to apply for.
    *   Upload your resume.
    *   *Target:* Ensure your score is **>60 in the skills section**.

---

# Activity: Draft Your Stand-Out Resume

1.  **Format:** Pick a clean, single-column template.
2.  **Sections:** Outline your sections based on the Fresher structure.
3.  **Drafting:** Write your project bullet points using Action Verbs and the STAR method.
4.  **Evaluate:** Run your draft through an ATS scanner later today!
