# Resume Building Masterclass

A resume is a key document in the professional world, serving as a snapshot of your qualifications, skills, experience, and accomplishments. 

Today, we will build a stand-out resume that gets you shortlisted.

---

# Features of a Good Resume

Before we start building, here is what makes a resume stand out:
*   **Clickable Links:** All necessary links (Email, LinkedIn, GitHub, Portfolio) are present and clickable.
*   **Concise Experiences:** All experiences and projects are mentioned in a concise, bulleted manner.
*   **Categorized Skills:** Skills are categorized (Languages, Tools, Databases, Web Technologies) and mentioned within project descriptions.
*   **Complete General Info:** Name, contact details, and location are clear.
*   **Proof of Work:** Mentions open-source projects (if any) and certifications with clickable links.

---

# Step 1: General Info & Portfolios

Your contact section must be flawless. Include:
*   **Full Name**
*   **Phone Number** & **Email Address** (Professional format)
*   **LinkedIn Profile (Must)**
*   **GitHub Profile (Must)** - Provides evidence of coding abilities, problem-solving skills, and open-source collaboration. Tech companies rely on it!
*   **Portfolio / Leetcode (Optional but recommended)**

---

# Step 2: Summary vs. Objective

**Resume Summary (For Working Professionals):**
A broader overview of your professional experience, skills, and accomplishments. It focuses on showcasing what you've achieved.
*Example:* "Results-oriented software engineer with 3 years of experience specializing in full-stack development..."

**Resume Objective (For Freshers):**
A concise statement outlining your career goals and the type of position you are seeking.
*Example:* "A motivated individual having core knowledge of web technologies, looking forward to working in a multi-national company to add value..."

---

# Step 3: Experience & Projects

For every role or project, ensure you have these 5 elements:
1.  **Job Title / Project Name**
2.  **Company Name / Demo Link**
3.  **Dates:** Start and end dates (or "Present")
4.  **Bullet Points:** Use the **STAR Method** (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to write these. Start with **Action Verbs** (Developed, Managed, Integrated).
5.  **Quantify Achievements:** Always use numbers (e.g., "Leading to a 30% increase in performance").
*Ensure consistency of grammar throughout!*

---

# Structure of the Resume

How you order your sections depends on your background:

**For Freshers / Non-Working Graduates:**
1.  Name, Contact, and Portfolio Links
2.  Skills (Tech Stacks)
3.  Education
4.  Projects based on relevance (Main & Mini)
5.  Clubs, Activities & Achievements

**For Working Professionals:**
1.  Name, Contact, and Portfolio Links
2.  Skills (Tech Stacks)
3.  Work Experience
4.  Projects
5.  Education & Achievements

---

# Evaluating Your Resume

Once your draft is ready, you must evaluate it:
1.  **Peer Review:** Request a review from colleagues, friends, or mentors.
2.  **ATS Score Check:**
    *   Open **resumeworded.com**
    *   Copy/Paste a Job Description you want to apply for.
    *   Upload your resume.
    *   *Target:* Ensure your score is **>60 in the skills section**.

---

# Activity: Draft Your Stand-Out Resume

1.  **Format:** Pick a clean, single-column template.
2.  **Sections:** Outline your sections based on the Fresher or Professional structure.
3.  **Drafting:** Write your project and experience bullet points using Action Verbs and the STAR method.
4.  **Evaluate:** Run your draft through an ATS scanner later today!

**Task:** You have 30 minutes to draft the first version of your professional resume.
