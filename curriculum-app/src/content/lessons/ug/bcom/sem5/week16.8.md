# Interview Preparation Strategies

An interview is not an interrogation. It is a professional conversation to determine mutual fit.

Today, we decode the psychology behind interviews.

---

# Types of Interviews

### 1. The HR Round (Behavioral)
Focuses on your personality, attitude, and culture fit.
*   "Tell me about yourself."
*   "What are your strengths and weaknesses?"

### 2. The Technical Round
Focuses on your core domain knowledge and problem-solving skills.
*   "How would you solve this scenario?"
*   "Explain [Core Concept] to a 5-year-old."

### 3. The Managerial Round
Focuses on leadership, stress management, and long-term goals.
*   "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
*   "Tell me about a time you handled a conflict."

---

# The STAR Method for Behavioral Questions

When asked a situational question (e.g., "Tell me about a time when..."), use **STAR**:

*   **S - Situation:** Set the scene and give the necessary context of your example.
*   **T - Task:** Describe what your responsibility was in that situation.
*   **A - Action:** Explain exactly what steps *you* took to address it.
*   **R - Result:** Share what outcomes your actions achieved (use numbers if possible).

---

# Activity: Mock Interview Pair Practice

1.  **Pair Up:** Find a partner.
2.  **Roleplay (10 mins each):**
    *   **Person A (Interviewer):** Ask 3 behavioral questions.
    *   **Person B (Candidate):** Answer using the STAR method.
3.  **Feedback (5 mins):** Interviewer gives constructive feedback on body language, clarity, and structure.
4.  **Switch Roles!**
