## Meetings and Teamwork

---

# Speaking in Meetings

![Speaking in Meetings](/images/sem3_w12_speaking.jpg)

Meetings can be intimidating, but they are your primary opportunity to demonstrate your value to the team.

*   **Prepare:** Never attend a meeting without reviewing the agenda. Have at least one point or question prepared.
*   **Speak Up Early:** It gets harder to speak the longer you wait. Make a small contribution early in the meeting to break the ice.
*   **Be Concise:** Make your point quickly and clearly. Do not ramble.

---

# Handling Interruptions

![Interruptions](/images/sem3_w12_interruptions.jpg)

Interruptions happen. How you handle them defines your professional presence.

*   **When you are interrupted:** Remain calm. You can say politely but firmly, "Please let me finish my point," or "I was just getting to that."
*   **When you need to interrupt:** Do so respectfully. "If I may add a quick point here..." or "Before we move on, I'd like to clarify..."
*   **Active Listening:** Ensure you are truly listening to your teammates, not just waiting for your turn to speak.

## Interpersonal Skills Focus: Conducting Productive Study Meetings

![Strategy](/assets/strategy.jpg)

Group study sessions can easily devolve into wasting time. Follow these rules:
1.  **Prepare**: Define a strict agenda (e.g., "Today we outline Chapter 3").
2.  **Conduct**: Keep discussion on track and gently guide peers back to the topic if they get distracted.
3.  **Close**: Ensure everyone knows exactly what their action items are for the next meeting.
