## Body Language

![Week 11 Title](/images/slides/ug_week11.jpg)

While what you say and write is crucial, how you present yourself non-verbally often speaks louder. This week, we focus on controlling the signals your body sends.

---

# Posture and Presence

![Posture](/images/sem3_w11_posture.jpg)

Your posture dictates how others perceive your confidence and engagement. 

*   **Standing:** Stand tall with your shoulders back. Avoid crossing your arms, which can seem defensive or closed off.
*   **Sitting:** Sit up straight, leaning slightly forward to show interest. Do not slump or lean too far back.
*   **Space:** Occupy your space comfortably without shrinking or sprawling.

---

# Eye Contact and Gestures

![Eye Contact](/images/sem3_w11_eyes.jpg)

Effective eye contact builds trust, while controlled gestures add emphasis to your words.

*   **Eye Contact:** Aim to maintain eye contact for 60-70% of a conversation. If staring feels intense, look at the bridge of the person's nose.
*   **Gestures:** Use your hands to emphasize key points, but keep them within "the box" (between your chest and waist). Avoid fidgeting with pens, jewelry, or your face.
*   **Facial Expressions:** Ensure your face matches your message. A slight, genuine smile is universally welcoming.

## Interpersonal Skills Focus: The Dangers of Groupthink

![Technology](/assets/technology.jpg)

**Groupthink** occurs when peer pressure causes team members to withhold contrary opinions during a project. 
It results in ill-advised decisions because no one wanted to "rock the boat" or argue with the group's alpha. 
*How to avoid it*: Always encourage "devil's advocates" in your study groups and foster a climate where dissenting opinions are celebrated.
