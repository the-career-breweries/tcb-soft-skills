# How to Enter a Discussion

![Entering a Discussion](/images/sem2_w2_entering.jpg)

One of the hardest parts of a group discussion is finding the right moment to speak without aggressively interrupting. 

*   **The "Yes, and..." Technique:** Build on someone else's point. "I agree with Sarah's point on X, and I'd like to add..."
*   **The Clarifying Question:** Enter by asking for depth. "That's an interesting perspective. How do you think that impacts Y?"
*   **Body Language:** Lean forward, make eye contact, and take a small breath in to visually signal you are about to speak.

# Active Listening in Groups

![Active Listening](/images/sem2_w2_listening.jpg)

Group discussions are not just about who speaks the loudest. Evaluators and managers watch who listens the best. 

When you are not speaking:
*   Maintain eye contact with the speaker.
*   Nod to show comprehension.
*   Take brief notes if appropriate.
*   Never look at your phone or stare at the ceiling.

# Activity: Structured GD Round

![GD Activity](/images/sem2_w2_activity.jpg)

Today, we will conduct a highly structured Group Discussion round. 

1.  **Form Pods:** Break into groups of 4-5 students.
2.  **The Prompt:** Your instructor will provide a general, non-controversial topic (e.g., "The impact of social media on study habits").
3.  **The Rules:** Every person MUST speak exactly twice. You must use a "Yes, and..." technique at least once.

# Evaluation Rubric

![Rubric](/images/sem2_w1_rubric.jpg)

You will be evaluated this week on:

*   **Participation:** Did you speak your required two times?
*   **Listening:** Did your body language show active listening when others spoke?
*   **Relevance:** Did your points connect logically to what the previous speaker said?
