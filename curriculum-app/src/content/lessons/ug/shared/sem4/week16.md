# Digital Etiquette

![Digital Etiquette](/images/sem4_w16_etiquette.jpg)

The rules of professional conduct apply just as strictly online as they do in person.

*   **Responsiveness:** Acknowledge receipts of important emails or messages within 24 hours, even if it's just to say, "I've received this and will get back to you by tomorrow."
*   **Context Switching:** Understand the difference between platforms. Slack/Teams is for quick, informal collaboration. Email is for formal requests, external communication, and documentation.
*   **The "Reply All" Rule:** Use "Reply All" sparingly. Only include people who genuinely need to see your response.

---

# File Discipline

![File Discipline](/images/sem4_w16_files.jpg)

Nothing screams "unprofessional" louder than sending an email with the subject "Resume" and an attached file named `resume_final_v3_real_FINAL.pdf`.

*   **Naming Conventions:** Name your files clearly before sending them. Include your name and the document type (e.g., `JohnDoe_Resume_2026.pdf`).
*   **Formats:** Unless specifically asked for a Word document, always send your professional documents (like resumes) as PDFs to preserve formatting.
*   **Attachments:** Mention the attachment in the body of your email so the recipient knows to look for it.
