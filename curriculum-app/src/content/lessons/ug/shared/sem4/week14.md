# Welcome to Semester 4: Week 14
## Interview Basics

![Week 14 Title](/images/sem4_w14_title.jpg)

The interview is the gateway to your career. It is not an interrogation; it is a conversation to determine mutual fit. This week, we cover the fundamentals of interview preparation.

---

# The Interview Process

![Interview Process](/images/sem4_w14_process.jpg)

Interviews rarely happen in a single step. Be prepared for a multi-stage process.

*   **The Phone Screen:** Usually a short call with HR to verify your basic qualifications and salary expectations.
*   **The Technical/Skills Round:** A deeper dive into your specific hard skills, often involving a test or portfolio review.
*   **The Cultural Fit/Manager Round:** A conversation with your potential boss to see if your working style aligns with the team.

---

# Preparation is Everything

![Preparation](/images/sem4_w14_prep.jpg)

Confidence in an interview comes from preparation, not just natural charisma.

*   **Research the Company:** Know what they do, their recent news, and their core values. "I like your culture" is a bad answer if you can't explain *why*.
*   **Know Your Resume:** Be ready to speak in detail about *anything* written on your resume. If you wrote it, you own it.
*   **Prepare Questions:** Always have questions for the interviewer. "What does a typical day look like?" or "How is success measured in this role?"

Let's start preparing for your first interview.

1.  **Target Company:** Pick a real company you would like to work for.
2.  **Research:** Use your phone or laptop to find their mission statement and one piece of recent news about them.
3.  **The "Tell Me About Yourself" Pitch:** Draft a 60-second introduction focusing on your education, relevant skills, and why you are interested in this field.
