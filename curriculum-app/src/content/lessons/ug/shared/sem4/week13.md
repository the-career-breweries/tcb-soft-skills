# Understanding Career Paths

![Career Paths](/images/sem4_w13_paths.jpg)

Your degree does not lock you into a single job title. Most modern careers are non-linear. 

*   **Vertical vs. Lateral:** You can move "up" into management, or "across" into different specializations (e.g., from Sales to Marketing).
*   **Industry vs. Function:** You might love the *Function* (e.g., Accounting) but want to change the *Industry* (e.g., from a bank to a tech startup).
*   **Continuous Learning:** The skills that get you your first job will not be the skills that get you your third job. 

---

# Aligning Interests and Reality

![Alignment](/images/sem4_w13_alignment.jpg)

"Follow your passion" is incomplete advice. A successful career is the intersection of what you like, what you are good at, and what the market actually pays for.

*   **Self-Assessment:** Look back at your Semester 1 profile. What are your core strengths? 
*   **Market Research:** What roles are currently in demand in your field? What are the entry-level requirements?
*   **The Gap Analysis:** What skills do you currently lack to get the job you want? How will you acquire them?

![Activity](/images/sem4_w13_activity.jpg)

It is time to put your goals on paper.

1.  **Drafting:** Fill out the Career Mapping worksheet. Identify two potential entry-level roles that interest you.
2.  **Gap Analysis:** For each role, identify one technical skill and one soft skill you need to improve to be a strong candidate.
3.  **Action Plan:** Write down one concrete step you can take *this month* to build one of those skills.
