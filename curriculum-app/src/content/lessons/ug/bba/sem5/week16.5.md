# Core Concept 1: Self-Awareness

The foundation of all soft skills is self-awareness. If you don't know your strengths and weaknesses, you cannot improve them.

We use a tool called the **Johari Window** to map this out:

<!-- PRINT: JohariWindow -->

| The Johari Window | Known to Self | Unknown to Self |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Known to Others** | **OPEN AREA**: Traits you and others both see. | **BLIND SPOT**: Traits others see, but you don't. |
| **Unknown to Others** | **HIDDEN AREA**: Secrets or fears you keep from others. | **UNKNOWN AREA**: Untapped potential. |

Your goal this semester is to expand your **Open Area** by reducing your Blind Spots (through feedback) and reducing your Hidden Area (by building confidence).

# Core Concept 2: Group Discussions

In Semesters 1 and 2, we covered the basics of Group Discussions. Here are the golden rules:

<!-- PRINT: GD1Checklist -->

* **Quality over Quantity:** Speaking for 5 minutes of nonsense is worse than 30 seconds of high-impact insight.
* **Don't just disagree, build:** Use phrases like *"Adding to what John said..."* or *"I see your point, however we must also consider..."*
* **Include others:** If someone is quiet, a great leader will say, *"Sarah, you haven't shared yet. What are your thoughts on this?"*

# Core Concept 3: Professional Writing

In Semester 3, we focused on how you present yourself on paper and online.

<!-- PRINT: WritingSkills1 -->

**Resume Basics:**
* **No typos.** A single typo can send your resume to the trash.
* **Use Action Verbs:** Instead of "was responsible for organizing", use *"Organized and managed..."*
* **Quantify:** Instead of "increased sales", use *"Increased sales by 15% in 3 months."*

**Email Etiquette:**
* Always use a clear, concise subject line.
* Keep emails brief; use bullet points for readability.
* Always include a professional sign-off.

# Core Concept 4: The STAR Method

In Semester 4, we introduced the ultimate tool for answering interview questions: **The STAR Method**.

When an interviewer says, *"Tell me about a time you faced a challenge,"* you must answer in this structure:

<!-- PRINT: StarResponse -->

* **S - Situation:** Set the scene (briefly). *"In my final year project..."*
* **T - Task:** What was your specific responsibility? *"I was in charge of the database."*
* **A - Action:** What did YOU do to solve the problem? *"I researched three NoSQL options and migrated the data..."*
* **R - Result:** What was the outcome? *"Our query speed increased by 40%."*

*Always spend the most time on the ACTION and RESULT.*

---

# STAR Method: A Full Example

**Question:** *"Tell me about a time you had to solve a complex problem."*

* **(S) Situation:** *"During a corporate finance competition, our team had to analyze the financial health of a struggling retail company."*
* **(T) Task:** *"We were tasked with identifying the root cause of their declining profit margins and proposing a turnaround strategy within 48 hours."*
* **(A) Action:** *"I took the lead on the financial modeling. I combed through their last 3 years of cash flow statements and identified a massive inventory bloat. I then collaborated with the team to propose a just-in-time (JIT) inventory model and calculated the projected cost savings."*
* **(R) Result:** *"Our presentation won 2nd place out of 20 teams. The judges specifically commended the depth of our financial modeling and the practicality of the JIT inventory solution."*
