## Data vs. Narrative

![Communication](/assets/communication.jpg)

**Data** tells you *what* happened. (e.g., "Operating expenses increased by 14% Q/Q").
**Narrative** tells you *why* it happened, and *what we should do next*.

Executives do not want a data dump. They want an insight.

**The Golden Rule of Financial Presentations:**
Never show a table when a chart will do. Never show a chart without a headline that states the exact takeaway.

---

## The "So What?" Framework

![Global](/assets/global.jpg)

When presenting financial data to non-finance stakeholders (Marketing, HR, Ops), always pass your presentation through the **"So What?"** filter.

1. **The Metric:** "Our Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) rose by $45."
2. **The Impact:** "This means our current marketing channels are becoming saturated and less profitable."
3. **The Actionable Insight:** "We need to reallocate $50k from paid ads into organic SEO this quarter to stabilize margins."

If your slide doesn't have an actionable insight, delete it.

---

## Visualizing Complexity

![Technology](/assets/technology.jpg)

You must master visual hierarchy.

*   **Highlighting:** Gray out all secondary data. Use one bright color (like deep blue or green) exclusively to highlight the exact number you are talking about.
*   **Decluttering:** Remove gridlines, unnecessary decimal points, and 3D effects from your charts.
*   **The 3-Second Rule:** The audience should understand the main point of your slide within 3 seconds.
