## Negotiation for Finance Professionals

![Strategy](/assets/strategy.jpg)

In commerce, you don't get what you deserve; you get what you negotiate.

Whether you are securing a vendor contract, finalizing an M&A deal, or fighting for your own starting salary, negotiation is the highest-leverage soft skill you can possess.

---

## The Concept of Leverage

![Global](/assets/global.jpg)

Negotiation is not about who is louder. It is entirely about leverage. 
**Leverage is simply the answer to one question:** *Who needs this deal more?*

*   **BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement):** Before you enter any negotiation, you must know exactly what you will do if the deal falls apart. If your BATNA is strong (e.g., you have 3 other job offers), your leverage is high. If you have no BATNA, you have no leverage.
*   **The Power of Walking Away:** The person who is most willing to walk away controls the negotiation. 

---

## Hard-Bargaining Tactics

![Operations](/assets/operations.jpg)

As a finance professional, you will face vendors and contractors who are trained to extract maximum margin from your company.

1. **The Flinch:** Always physically or verbally react with surprise to the first offer, no matter what it is. A flinch signals that their expectation is completely misaligned with your budget.
2. **The Vise:** Use the phrase, *"You'll have to do better than that,"* and then remain completely silent. The silence will make them uncomfortable, and they will often negotiate against themselves.
3. **Bracketing:** Never give a single number. Give a range where the *top* of your range is your actual target.

## Salary Negotiation

![Career](/assets/career.jpg)

The biggest financial mistake you can make is accepting the first salary offer out of graduation.

1. **Deflect the Initial Question:** If asked for your expected salary during early interviews, deflect: *"I'm currently focused on finding the right fit, but I'm sure if the fit is right, we can come to a competitive agreement based on market rates."*
2. **Make Them Anchor First:** Try to get them to state a number first.
3. **The "Disappointed Silence":** When they give you an offer, pause. Look slightly disappointed. Let the silence hang for 5 seconds before responding.

## Today's Deliverable

**The Procurement Simulation:**

You are the Head of Procurement. A SaaS vendor is trying to renew their annual contract at $120,000 (a 20% increase from last year). You know their direct competitor is offering a similar product for $90,000, but migrating to the new system would cause 3 weeks of operational downtime.

**Your Task:**
1. Draft a script for the opening 2 minutes of your renegotiation call.
2. Identify your exact BATNA.
3. What is the maximum price you are willing to pay, and what is your opening counter-offer?
