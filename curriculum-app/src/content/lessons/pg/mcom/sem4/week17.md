## Path-Specific Execution

![Career](/assets/career.jpg)

You survived the interview. You got the offer. Now the real test begins.

The first 90 days on a finance desk will define your trajectory at that firm. If you stumble out of the gate, you will be branded as a "B-player" and given mundane tasks. If you crush it, you get put on the high-profile deals.

---

## Surviving Your First 90 Days

Depending on the persona you chose in Semester 3, your success metrics look very different.

### 1. The Big 4 Auditor
*   **The Reality:** You will be placed in a "bullpen" with 20 other fresh hires. You will be given a massive stack of Vouching and Tracing tasks that make no sense.
*   **The 90-Day Strategy:** Do not ask the Senior Manager how to do your job. Spend 15 minutes trying to solve the problem yourself, write down your proposed solution, and then ask a 2nd-year Associate for confirmation. Accuracy is more important than speed. Never make the same formatting mistake twice.

### 2. The Corporate Finance Strategist
*   **The Reality:** You will be drowning in ERP system data. Operations will hate you because you are tracking their spending. 
*   **The 90-Day Strategy:** Build alliances outside of finance. Take the Head of Marketing or the Supply Chain Manager out for coffee. If you understand *how* the business makes money on the ground, your financial models will be 10x more accurate than your peers who just stare at Excel.

### 3. The Investment Banker / PE Analyst
*   **The Reality:** 80 to 100-hour work weeks. Extreme sleep deprivation. You will be asked to re-format pitch books at 2 AM.
*   **The 90-Day Strategy:** "Plugging" is a fireable offense (hardcoding a number to make the balance sheet balance). Never do it. Build a reputation for flawless attention to detail. Always carry a notepad; never make a VP tell you a formatting instruction twice.
