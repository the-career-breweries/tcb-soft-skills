## The Technical Interview Defense

![Communication](/assets/communication.jpg)

Welcome to Semester 4. This is the final launchpad.

The biggest hurdle between you and a high-paying finance role is the Technical Interview. 
Finance interviews are deliberately designed to be hostile. They want to see if you crack under pressure before they put you in front of a client or let you touch a live model.

---

## Anatomy of the "Grilling"

![Operations](/assets/operations.jpg)

You will be placed in a room with 2-4 senior professionals. They will rapid-fire technical questions at you. When you answer, they will interrupt you and tell you that you are wrong (even if you are right).

**Why do they do this?**
They are testing your *conviction*. If you immediately back down and apologize when challenged on a correct answer, you fail the test. 

**How to Handle the Grill:**
1. **The Pause:** Never answer immediately. Take 3 seconds. It shows you are thinking, not panicking.
2. **The Clarifying Question:** If the prompt is vague (e.g., "Walk me through an LBO"), narrow the scope before answering: *"Are we assuming a standard 5-year exit horizon with a 20% IRR target?"*
3. **The Pivot on Failure:** If you do not know the answer, DO NOT GUESS. Say: *"I haven't encountered that specific depreciation schedule, but based on MACRS logic, I would assume..."* Show your thought process.

## The Case Study Defense

![Strategy](/assets/strategy.jpg)

You will often be given a 48-hour take-home case study (e.g., modeling a merger or auditing a messy trial balance) and then asked to present it.

*   **Own the Mistakes:** You will make a mistake in your model. When they point it out, do not get defensive. Say: *"Good catch. Let's trace how that flows through to the cash flow statement."*
*   **Defend the Assumptions:** Your final number doesn't matter as much as the assumptions you used to get there. If you projected 5% YoY growth, be ready to defend *why* it's 5% and not 4% or 6%.
