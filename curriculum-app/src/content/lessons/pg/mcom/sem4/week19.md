## The Compensation Portfolio

![Finance](/assets/finance.jpg)

As you move into higher tiers of finance, your "Base Salary" becomes the least interesting part of your compensation package. 

Wealth in finance is not built on bi-weekly paychecks. It is built on equity, performance bonuses, and carried interest. You must know how to value them.

---

## The Elements of High-End Compensation

![Strategy](/assets/strategy.jpg)

### 1. The Performance Bonus
In Investment Banking and Private Equity, your year-end bonus can be 50% to 100%+ of your base salary.
*   **The Trap:** Banks will "claw back" your bonus if you quit within a certain timeframe. Never spend your bonus before it clears your bank account.
*   **The Strategy:** Treat your base salary as your living expense budget. Treat your bonus entirely as investment capital.

### 2. Equity & Vesting Cliffs
Startups and tech-forward finance roles will offer RSUs (Restricted Stock Units) or Options.
*   **The Cliff:** A standard vesting schedule is "4-year vest, 1-year cliff." This means if you quit or are fired on day 364, you get *zero* equity.
*   **The Strategy:** Always calculate the "Expected Value" of the equity (which means factoring in the 90% chance the startup fails).

### 3. Carried Interest ("Carry")
In Private Equity and Venture Capital, "Carry" is a share of the profits of an investment fund. This is how billionaires are made. It aligns your incentives perfectly with the LPs (Limited Partners).

**The Compensation Breakdown:**

**Offer A (Big 4 Bank):** $110,000 Base, $40,000 guaranteed year-end cash bonus.

1. Calculate the guaranteed Year 1 cash for both.
2. Outline the risks associated with Offer B's equity.
3. Which offer do you take, and why?
