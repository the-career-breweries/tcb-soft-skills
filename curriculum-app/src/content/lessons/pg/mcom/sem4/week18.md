## High-Stakes Crisis Simulation

![Time Management](/assets/time_management.jpg)

You can plan a perfect financial model, but you cannot plan the market.

At some point in your career, an external macroeconomic shock will occur. A central bank will unexpectedly hike interest rates, a global pandemic will freeze supply chains, or a major geopolitical event will crash the equity markets.

When the market crashes, your firm's executives will turn to you for answers.

---

## The "Margin Call" War Room

![Global](/assets/global.jpg)

**The Scenario:**
It is 7:00 AM on a Tuesday. News just broke that a major global bank has collapsed overnight due to toxic asset exposure. The S&P 500 futures are down 6%. Credit markets have completely frozen. 

Your CFO has called an emergency war-room meeting in 2 hours. Your company has $50M in short-term commercial paper rolling over next week, and the credit markets are shut.

**The Rules of Crisis Triage:**
1. **Cash is King:** During a shock, P&L (profitability) ceases to matter. The only thing that matters is Liquidity (Cash Flow). Do we have enough cash to make payroll on Friday?
2. **Speed over Precision:** The CFO does not need a 5-decimal-point accurate model. They need a directional estimate *right now*. Is the exposure $5M or $50M?
3. **No Hysteria:** Panic is contagious. Speak slower than usual. Present facts, not fears.

## Today's Deliverable
<!-- PRINT: CrisisTriageShock -->

**The War Room Briefing:**

You have exactly 2 hours to prepare the briefing for the CFO.

1. **Liquidity Assessment:** Identify the 3 immediate levers you can pull to preserve cash (e.g., freezing hiring, delaying vendor payments, tapping a revolver credit line).
2. **Exposure Mapping:** Which of your major clients or suppliers are most likely to go bankrupt because of this banking collapse?
3. **The Executive Summary:** Write the exact 3-sentence opening statement you will use when the CFO walks into the room. (It must be calm, factual, and action-oriented).
