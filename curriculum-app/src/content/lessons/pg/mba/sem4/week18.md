Business is not a straight line.

Today, we test how you break, and how you rebuild.

## Welcome to the War Room

For the next 45 minutes, you are no longer in a classroom. 

You are the executive team of a rapidly failing enterprise. 
The scenario is live, the clock is ticking, and every decision has immediate, psychological consequences.

---

## Simulation Mechanics: "The Death Spiral"

![Time Management](/assets/time_management.jpg)

**The Setup:**
1. You will be grouped into pods of 5.
2. The facilitator will hand you a sealed envelope containing your "Crisis Zero" (e.g., Your primary manufacturer just went bankrupt, or a key executive was just indicted).
3. **You have 10 minutes** to draft an initial response strategy.

---

## The Sensitization Curve

As you work, the facilitator will introduce **injects** (sudden variables) every 10 minutes:
*   *Inject 1:* A major news outlet just leaked your internal emails.
*   *Inject 2:* Your lead investor is pulling out in 24 hours.
*   *Inject 3:* Half your staff is staging a walkout.

Notice your heart rate. Notice who in your team takes charge, who panics, and who goes quiet. 

---

## Psychological Resilience

![Body Language](/assets/body_language.jpg)

Crisis management is 20% strategy and 80% emotional regulation.

*   **The Freeze Response:** Paralyzed by too many bad options.
*   **The Flight Response:** Blaming others or abandoning the core mission.
*   **The Fight Response:** Making aggressive, overly risky bets to quickly "fix" it.

Which one did you default to today?
