## The Digital Business

![Technology](/assets/technology.jpg)


Every company is now a technology company. Whether you are in FMCG, Logistics, or Healthcare, digital transformation is the core driver of growth.

This session explores the intersection of business strategy and modern technology.

---

## 1. Digital Transformation (DX)

![Technology](/assets/technology.jpg)


DX is not about buying new software; it is a fundamental rewiring of how an organization operates and delivers value.

**The Three Pillars of DX:**
1.  **Customer Experience:** Digitizing touchpoints, personalizing interactions.
2.  **Operational Process:** Automating workflows, data-driven performance management.
3.  **Business Models:** Transitioning from physical to digital, products to subscriptions.

---

## 2. Artificial Intelligence in Business

![Technology](/assets/technology.jpg)


AI is rapidly shifting from experimental to operational.

**Core Applications:**
*   **Predictive Analytics:** Forecasting demand, predicting churn.
*   **Hyper-Personalization:** Tailoring marketing at scale (MarTech).
*   **Process Automation:** RPA (Robotic Process Automation) in finance and HR.

*Executive Question: How will AI disrupt the profit pool of your target industry within the next 3 years?*

---

## 3. Tech Strategy Framework

![Technology](/assets/technology.jpg)


When evaluating new technology, executives must avoid the "shiny object syndrome."

**The Build vs. Buy vs. Partner Decision:**
*   **Build:** Does this technology provide a core competitive advantage?
*   **Buy:** Is this a standard capability where off-the-shelf software suffices (e.g., HRIS, basic CRM)?
*   **Partner:** Can we integrate with an ecosystem to accelerate time-to-market?

*Task: Analyze a recent digital acquisition by a Fortune 500 company using this framework.*

## Executive Interpersonal Skills: Leading Digital Change

![Technology](/assets/technology.jpg)

When spearheading digital transformation, resistance to change is your biggest enemy.
*   **Empathetic Leadership:** Understand why employees fear AI or automation.
*   **Clear Communication:** Frame technological shifts not as 'replacements' but as 'enhancements' to the human workforce.
