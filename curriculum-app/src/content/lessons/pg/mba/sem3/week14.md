## Building the Foundation

![Strategy](/assets/strategy.jpg)


Whether launching a startup, spinning off a new corporate division, or restructuring a department, you must know how to build from the ground up.

This session covers the foundational architecture of new ventures.

---

## 1. Finding Product-Market Fit (PMF)

![Marketing](/assets/marketing.jpg)


PMF is the holy grail of business. It means you are in a good market with a product that can satisfy that market.

**Indicators of PMF:**
*   Customers are buying the product as fast as you can make it.
*   Usage is growing organically (word of mouth).
*   High retention rates and low churn.

*The Lean Startup methodology emphasizes building a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) to test PMF quickly.*

---

## 2. Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

![Marketing](/assets/marketing.jpg)


A great product fails without a strategy to put it in the hands of customers.

**Key Components:**
*   **Target Audience:** Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) and Buyer Personas.
*   **Value Proposition:** What specific pain point are you solving?
*   **Pricing Strategy:** Penetration, Skimming, or Value-based?
*   **Distribution Channels:** Direct sales, partnerships, e-commerce?

---

## 3. Unit Economics & Scaling

![Finance](/assets/finance.jpg)


Before scaling, you must ensure the foundation is profitable at the unit level.

**The Golden Ratio:**
*   **LTV (Life Time Value):** How much gross margin do you make from a customer over their lifetime?
*   **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** How much does it cost to acquire them?
*   *Rule of thumb: LTV:CAC should be at least 3:1.*

*Scaling a business with broken unit economics just burns capital faster.*


---

## Executive Interpersonal Skills: Influencing Investors

![Finance](/assets/finance.jpg)

Whether pitching internally for a budget or externally to VCs:
*   **The Narrative Arc:** Data alone doesn't persuade; stories do. Frame your PMF and GTM metrics within a compelling narrative.
*   **Confidence Under Fire:** Practice fielding aggressive Q&A sessions without becoming defensive.
